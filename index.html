Who we serve

Reaching youth who need a safe place to turn.

Traditional programmes rely on classrooms, invitations, and forms — meaning the young people who need support most are the first to be left out. We meet youth right where they are: on the field. MYEI focuses on:

  • School dropouts and youth who can't return to school because of fees.
  • Young people without steady mentors, role models, or a safe peer group.
  • Coming next — a dedicated programme to safely reach and support girls in our community who are at risk of early marriage.

How we work

One pitch, three things happening.

Football isn't a side activity — it's the structure everything else runs on. Each step builds on the trust and routine of the one before it.

01 Active now

Football as the point of contact

Regular matches and training sessions give at-risk youth a reason to show up — consistently, for free, no questions asked. It builds trust, teamwork, and physical health.

02 Building now

Mentorship, off the ball

After sessions, we use the trust already built to open conversations that matter — sexual and reproductive health, mental wellness, and navigating pressure as a young person.

03 Phase II

Peer-to-peer skills training

Our planned next milestone: CBO members who already hold a trade skill lead short courses for their peers in things like electrical wiring, plumbing, and coding.
